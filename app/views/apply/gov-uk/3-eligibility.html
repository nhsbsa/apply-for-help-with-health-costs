3. Eligibility

The amount of savings, investments or property you are allowed before you can't get help with NHS costs differs depending on where you live.

Where you live Total savings, investments or property
(not including the property you live in)
Not in a care home £16,000
Permanently living in a care home in England £23,250
Permanently living in a care home in Scotland £23,250
Permanently living in a care home in Wales £24,000

Check if you already qualify you for full help with your NHS costs. If you do, you don't need to apply.

Already qualify for help paying NHS costs

Claiming certain benefits

You are already entitled to full help with your NHS costs if you or your partner are getting:

  • Income Support
  • Income-based Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)
  • Income-related Employment Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Pension Credit Guarantee Credit
  • Universal Credit, depending on your earnings
  • Child Tax Credit or Working Tax Credit with the disability element, with a household income of £15,276 or less

Your partner and any children under 20 included on your benefits claim are also entitled to full help with NHS costs.

Asylum seekers

If you are still waiting for your asylum decision from the Home Office, you will get full help with NHS costs, and should be issued a Full help Health Certificate (HC2) by UK Visas and Immigration.

Your situation What happens
Awaiting asylum decision You'll be issued a Full help Health Certificate (HC2)
Awaiting outcome of an appeal You'll be issued a Full help Health Certificate (HC2)
Refused permission to stay You need to apply
Given permission to stay You need to apply
