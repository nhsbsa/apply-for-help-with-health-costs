You can't apply for help with NHS costs yet

Why can't I apply

Because you're waiting for the decision to be made about your Universal Credit claim, we don't know whether you should apply through this service or if you would be automatically entitled to help with your NHS Costs.

What to do next

You need a full Universal Credit assessment period to claim help with NHS costs.

Prescriptions

You should pay for your prescription and request an 'FP57' refund receipt, which is an official receipt issued by the chemist.

The NHS prescription charge is currently £8.60 for each prescribed item, which means that there is a charge for each different drug or medical appliance on your prescription.

Once your first full assessment period has finished and you meet the qualifying conditions, take your FP57 refund receipt to your pharmacy, with evidence that you getting qualifying Universal Credit when you paid. You must do this within three months of the date that you paid. The pharmacy will refund you.

Dental charges

You should pay for your NHS dental treatment or check-up.

Once your first full assessment period has finished and you meet the qualifying conditions, you can claim a refund. You must do this within three months of the date that you paid.
