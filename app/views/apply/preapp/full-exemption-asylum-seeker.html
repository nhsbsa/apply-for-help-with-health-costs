Based on what you've told us You get help with NHS costs and don't need to apply

Because you are still waiting for your asylum decision or the outcome of an appeal:

You get free:

  • NHS prescriptions
  • NHS dental check-ups and treatment
  • sight tests
  • NHS wigs and fabric supports

You get money off:

  • new glasses or contact lenses
  • repairing or replacing your glasses or contact lenses
  • travel for NHS treatment

How to get your help

You should be issued a Full help Health Certificate (HC2) by UK Visas and Immigration.

Your certificate lasts 6 months and you'll continue to be sent a new one until the Home Office has decided on your application or appeal.

You don't have to do anything more, although you may have to show your Full help Health Certificate (HC2) to your pharmacist, optician or dentist.

Help with travel for NHS treatment

Tell the hospital (or the place where you’re getting treatment) that you want to claim back the cost of your journey. They’ll tell you what you need to do.

You may need to show proof:

  • of your travel costs, for example, bus tickets
  • that you’re entitled to help
