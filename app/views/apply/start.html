Apply for help to pay NHS costs

If you have difficulty paying your NHS costs you may be able to get help based on your circumstances.

When you don't need to apply

You are already entitled to full help with your NHS costs if you or your partner are getting:
  • Income Support
  • Income-based Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)
  • Income-related Employment Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Pension Credit Guarantee Credit
  • Universal Credit, depending on your earnings
  • Certain tax credits and the income on your award notice is £15,276 or less

Your partner and any children under 20 included on your benefits claim are also entitled to full help with NHS costs.

What to do if you get one of these benefits

You don't have to do anything although you may have to show proof that you get one of these benefits.You can use your award notice as proof.

Get a copy of yours by contacting the Jobcentre Plus Helpline or

If you need to apply for help

You could get help with your health costs if you, or you and your partner if you live as a couple, have less than:

  • £16,000 in savings, investments or properties – not including where you live
  • £23,250 in savings, investments or properties if you permanently live in a care home

You could get help towards your, your partner’s and your children’s:

  • NHS dental treatment
  • NHS prescriptions
  • NHS wigs and fabric supports
  • sight tests, glasses and contact lenses
  • travel to receive NHS treatment if you were referred by a GP, hospital doctor, dentist or optician

You can apply at any time. Do not wait until you need treatment.
