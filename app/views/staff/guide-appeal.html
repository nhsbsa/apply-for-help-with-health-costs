Introduction

An applicant may appeal if they disagree with the decision about their application for help with fees. However, if they've made a mistake, for example, they've stated their income incorrectly or not given all relevant information, you should recommend that they apply again rather than appeal.


Who can appeal

The appeal:
  • must be made within 14 days from the date they had to appeal
  • must include the original application along with any evidence or new information


What to do

If you receive an appeal:
  • Pass the appeal on to your delivery manager
  • The delivery manager will deal with the appeal

Successful appeals

If the delivery manager decides that the applicant’s appeal is valid, they will write to the applicant with the outcome.


Unsuccessful appeals

The delivery manager must write to the applicant within 10 days of receiving the appeal. The applicant may then appeal again to the operational manager.

The operational manager’s decision is final.
